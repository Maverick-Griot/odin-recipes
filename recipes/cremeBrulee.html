Creme Brulee

Description

An exquisitely rich and elegant dessert. Most crème brûlée recipes require the use of a small propane torch to achieve the crackly sugar top, but this version offers a simpler (and safer) solution: your oven's broiler. One thing to note: Be sure to let the custard set for several hours in the refrigerator before brûléeing the top, otherwise you'll end up with soupy custard.

  • Prep Time: 10 - 15 Minutes
  • Cook Time: 30 - 40 Minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 Cups of Heavy Cream
  • 1 Vanilla Bean or 1 Teaspoon of Vanilla Bean paste
  • Pinch of Salt
  • 5 Egg Yolks
  • 1/2 Cups of Sugar for the custard
  • Keep a small disk worth of sugar off to the side for the topping

Process

  1. Heat Oven to 325 Degrees and bring a pot of water to a rolling boil.
  2. In a saucepan combine cream, salt, vanilla bean(if you are using it) and cook over low heat just until hot. Let sit for a few minutes, then discard vanilla bean. If you are instead using vanilla bean paste, add it now.
  3. In a bowl, beat the yolks and sugar together until light. Stir about a quarter of the cream into this mixture, then pour sugar-egg mixture into cream and stir.
  4. Once everything is well incorporated fill your ramekins and place filled ramekins inside of a baking dish.
  5. With all the rameskins have been filled pour boiling water into the baking dish until the water is half way is halfway up the sides of the ramekins.
  6. Baked for 30-40 minutes. The center will have a slight jiggle. Place in refrigerator and allow to cool completely prior to adding the sugar garnish and serving.
  7. Sugar Garnish: Add a teaspoon of sugar to the top and spread into a thin layer. Either place in broiler for until browned or use a propane torch.
